if learned darkness from our searched world wrestest the rare unwisdom of thy eyes, if thy two hands flowers of silence curled upon a wish,to rapture should surprise my soul slowly which on thy beauty dreams (proud through the cold perfect night whisperless to mark,how that asleep whitely she seems whose lips the whole of life almost do guess) if god should send the morning;and before my doubting window leaves softly to stir, of thoughtful trees whom night hath pondered o’er —and frailties of dimension to occur about us and birds known,scarcely to sing (heart,could we bear the marvel of this thing?)
