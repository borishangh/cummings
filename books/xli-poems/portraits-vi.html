it’s just like a coffin’s inside when you die, pretentious and shiny and not too wide dear god there’s a portrait over the door very notable of the sultan’s nose pullable and rosy flanked by the scrumptious magdalene of whoisit and madame something by gainsborough just the playthings for dust n’es-ce pas effendi drifts between tables like an old leaf between toadstools he is the cheerfulest of men his peaked head smoulders like a new turd in April his legs are brittle and small his feet large and fragile his queer hands twitter before him,like foolish butterflies he is the most courteous of men should you remark the walls have been repapered he will nod like buddha or answer modestly i am dying so let us come in together and drink coffee covered with froth half-mud and not too sweet?
